Famous Myths

Many of these myths were shared across Europe, with variations in different cultures. For example, versions of The Wild Hunt appear in German, English, and Scandinavian folklore.

Cover images created with Gemini .

The Man in the Moon

In this myth, a man is condemned to the moon for stealing on a Sunday. This myth reveals medieval beliefs about sin, the afterlife, and the moon’s mystical qualities.

The Seven Sleepers of Ephesus

Seven Christian youths are said to have miraculously slept for centuries in a cave, awakening during a time of Christian persecution.

The Wild Hunt

A supernatural hunt led by ghostly figures, often viewed as a warning or omen. This tale embodies the medieval fear of the unknown and fascination with the supernatural.

Prester John

This is the story of a legendary Christian king and priest believed to rule over a vast, wealthy kingdom in the East.

The Dog Gellert

This Welsh legend tells the tragic story of Prince Llywelyn and his loyal dog, Gellert. Llywelyn returns home to find his infant’s cradle overturned and Gellert covered in blood.

The Mountain of Venus

The story of a knight, often identified as Tannhäuser, who becomes enchanted by the goddess Venus and is lured into her subterranean realm, the Mountain of Venus.

Deconstruct medieval legends

"Curious Myths of the Middle Ages" presents a collection of legends that explore human fears, hopes, and the supernatural. Baring-Gould recounts each tale, investigates its origins, and reveals how it reflects the values of medieval society.

This is a study project for CS50x . The book is in the public domain .
